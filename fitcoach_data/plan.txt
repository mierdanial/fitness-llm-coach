## WEEKLY WORKOUT PLAN  
**Structure:** 3‑day full‑body routine (Monday, Wednesday, Friday).  
Each session focuses on the same muscle groups but varies the primary lift to keep stimulus fresh.

| Day | Muscle Focus | Exercise (Sets × Reps) | Rest | Coaching Cue |
|-----|--------------|------------------------|------|--------------|
| **Mon** | Lower‑body & Core | 1. Goblet Squat – 3×10 | 90 s | “Keep your chest up and knees tracking over toes.” |
| | | 2. Push‑Up – 3×8‑10 | 60 s | “Drive through the heels, keep a straight line.” |
| | | 3. Seated Row (machine) – 3×10 | 60 s | “Pull the handle toward your lower ribs, squeeze the shoulder blades.” |
| | | 4. Plank – 3×30 s | 60 s | “Engage the core, avoid sagging hips.” |
| **Wed** | Upper‑body & Core | 1. Bench Press (light) – 3×10 | 90 s | “Lower the bar to mid‑chest, push through the palms.” |
| | | 2. Romanian Deadlift – 3×10 | 90 s | “Feel the stretch in the hamstrings, keep the back neutral.” |
| | | 3. Lat Pulldown – 3×10 | 60 s | “Pull the bar to the upper chest, keep elbows close.” |
| | | 4. Side Plank – 3×20 s each side | 60 s | “Keep the hips lifted, core tight.” |
| **Fri** | Full‑body & Core | 1. Front Squat (light) – 3×10 | 90 s | “Keep the bar on the front delts, knees over toes.” |
| | | 2. Incline Push‑Up – 3×10 | 60 s | “Hands slightly wider than shoulder‑width.” |
| | | 3. Dumbbell Row – 3×10 | 60 s | “Pull elbow toward the hip, keep the torso stable.” |
| | | 4. Bird‑Dog – 3×12 each side | 60 s | “Maintain a neutral spine, extend opposite arm/leg.” |

**Note on Knee Pain:**  
- Keep squat depth to a 90° knee angle or less.  
- Use a weight‑lifting belt for added core stability during squats and deadlifts.  
- If pain spikes, pause the exercise and perform a lighter variation (e.g., wall sit or step‑ups).

## PROGRESSIVE OVERLOAD STRATEGY  
**Goal:** Gradual increase in load or volume while maintaining form.  

| Week | Load Increase | Reps/Set Change | Rationale |
|------|---------------|-----------------|------------|
| 1–2 | 5 % above starting weight | 3×10 | Establish baseline, ensure proper technique. |
| 3–4 | 5 % above Week 2 weight | 3×10 | Add slight load, maintain reps. |
| 5–6 | 5 % above Week 4 weight | 3×12 | Increase volume to stimulate hypertrophy. |
| 7–8 | 5 % above Week 6 weight | 4×10 | Add a set, keep reps moderate. |
| 9–10 | 5 % above Week 8 weight | 4×12 | Further volume increase. |
| 11–12 | 5 % above Week 10 weight | 4×10 | Peak load before reassessment. |

**Implementation Tips:**  
- Record the weight used each session.  
- If you can complete the top rep with good form, add the next 5 % load next week.  
- If pain occurs, reduce load by 5 % and focus on form.

## NUTRITION GUIDE  
**Daily Calorie Target:** ~3 000 kcal (moderate surplus for muscle gain).  

| Macro | Target (g) | % of Total |
|-------|------------|------------|
| Protein | 112 g (1.6 g/kg) | 15 % |
| Carbohydrates | 280 g (4 g/kg) | 37 % |
| Fats | 56 g (0.8 g/kg) | 8 % |
| **Remaining Calories** | 1 432 kcal | 40 % (mostly carbs & fats) |

**Meal Timing:**  
- **Pre‑Workout (30–60 min):** 30 g carbs + 15 g protein (e.g., banana + whey shake).  
- **Post‑Workout (within 30 min):** 20 g protein + 30 g carbs (e.g., chicken breast + sweet potato).  
- **Evening:** Balanced meal with protein, carbs, and healthy fats.
